Why It Feels Hard

Content ideas feel hard for predictable reasons.

If you sit down to post and feel stuck, it is rarely because you have nothing to say. It is more often because of one of these patterns.

1

No clear niche

Without a defined direction, every idea feels equally valid — and equally hard to commit to.

2

Too many interests

Having many things to say can make it harder, not easier, to choose what to post.

3

Fear of judgment

Worrying about how a post will be perceived can stop it from being written at all.

4

No content pillars

Without repeatable themes, every post starts from zero, which is exhausting.

5

No proof bank

Without a record of your experiences, lessons and work, it is harder to draw on real material.

6

Copying others

Trying to sound like someone else can make your own ideas feel less worth sharing.

7

No audience clarity

Not knowing who you are writing for makes it difficult to choose tone, depth or topic.

The Knownly Framework

The Knownly content direction framework.

Your content direction comes from the intersection of what you know, what you have lived through, and what you want next — not from trends.

What you know

The knowledge, skills or expertise you can draw on without extra research.

What you have experienced

The situations, projects or challenges you have personally been through.

What people ask you for

The questions or advice people already come to you for, online or offline.

What you are learning

The topics you are currently exploring, which can make great "in progress" content.

What audience you want to attract

The kind of people you want reading your content and remembering you.

What opportunity you want

The direction — career, clients, audience or authority — your content should support.

Where to Start

5 types of posts you can start with.

You do not need a complex content calendar to begin. These five formats can be reused across almost any topic.

Lesson post

Share something you learned, and why it matters.

"Here's something I learned about [topic] this week, and why it changed how I think about [related idea]."

Mistake post

Share a mistake you made or commonly see, and what to do instead.

"A mistake I made (or often see) with [topic] — and what I'd do differently next time."

Breakdown post

Take something complex and explain it simply, step by step.

"Here's a simple breakdown of [topic] in [number] steps."

Story post

Share a real situation and the lesson it taught you.

"A few months ago, I [situation]. Here's what it taught me about [topic]."

Opinion post

Share a perspective on something happening in your field.

"Here's my take on [trend or topic] — and why I see it differently."
What to Avoid

Patterns that make posting harder, not easier.

Posting only quotes

Quotes from others rarely show what you specifically know or believe.

Copying trends blindly

Trends that do not fit your direction can dilute your signal even if they get attention.

Talking about everything

Without a few consistent themes, your audience may struggle to know what to expect from you.

Posting without profile clarity

Great posts can underperform if your profile does not explain who you are.

Overthinking every post

Waiting for the "perfect" post often means not posting at all.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

What should I post on LinkedIn if I don't know what to post?+

Start with lessons from your work, breakdowns of problems you understand, and observations from things you are currently learning or building.

What if I am just starting out and have no audience yet?+

Beginners can post about what they are learning, questions they are exploring, and small wins or lessons. You do not need an existing audience to start posting with direction.

Can I post if I don't feel like an expert yet?+

Yes. You can share what you are learning, document your process, or explain things from the perspective of someone slightly ahead of where your audience is.

How many content pillars should I have?+

Most people benefit from one primary theme and two supporting themes. This keeps your presence focused while still allowing some variety.

Will Knownly guarantee my content goes viral?+

No. Knownly does not guarantee virality, followers, clients or income. It helps you find a clearer content direction so your effort is more focused.
