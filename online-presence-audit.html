Common Gaps

What most people get wrong about their online presence.

These are the most common gaps Knownly sees between who someone is and how they appear online. Most are fixable once you understand the root cause.

Strong offline value, weak online signalYou have real experience and ability, but your profile does not reflect it clearly to someone who has never met you.
Posting without positioningYou create content regularly, but there is no consistent direction that connects your posts to one professional signal.
Strong skills, weak visible proofYour skills are real, but there is little public evidence of your work, outcomes or expertise for others to see.
Too many unrelated topicsYour profile and content cover too many areas, making it hard for anyone to understand what you should be remembered for.
No clear profile call to actionSomeone lands on your profile and does not know what to do next — there is no clear step, link or invitation.
Profile says one thing, content says anotherYour headline describes one direction while your posts move in a completely different direction — confusing anyone who visits.
No audience clarityYour profile is written as if it is for everyone, which means it resonates clearly with no one in particular.
No trust-building contentYour presence is professional but distant — there is nothing that helps people feel confident in reaching out to you.

FAQ

Common questions about online presence audits.

What is an online presence audit?
It is a review of how clearly your profiles, content, proof and public signals explain who you are and what people should remember you for. A good audit looks at clarity, consistency, trust and opportunity readiness — not just follower counts.
Is this only for LinkedIn?
No. Your online presence can include LinkedIn, Instagram, X, YouTube, a personal website, portfolio or other public profiles. Knownly looks at the platforms most relevant to your goal and stage.
Do I need a large audience for an audit?
No. In fact, an audit is especially useful before trying to grow, because it helps you fix clarity and positioning first. Growing on top of an unclear profile often leads to slower or weaker results.
Does Knownly review private accounts?
No. Knownly only works with the information and public links you choose to share. Do not submit passwords or private account access. All analysis is based on what you provide in your diagnosis and profile report.
Does an audit guarantee more clients or jobs?
No. An audit can improve your clarity, positioning and opportunity readiness, but Knownly does not guarantee jobs, clients, followers or income. What it gives you is a clearer direction and a more effective starting point.
