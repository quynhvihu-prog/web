Why This Matters

People do not remember everything you can do.

People remember the clearest repeated signal you show them. If your profile, content and conversations point in different directions, people may not understand where to place you — even if you are capable.

⚡

Skills without signal

Having skills matters. But without a clear public signal, those skills remain invisible to the people who could benefit from them.

🧭

Interests without direction

Many interests spread your attention across topics and make it harder for others to understand your area of authority.

📄

Content without positioning

Creating content without a clear positioning strategy can leave your audience confused about what you stand for.

👁️

Visibility without trust

Being seen is not the same as being trusted. Clarity in your direction builds trust over time with the right audience.

Knownly Archetypes

Examples of Knownly directions.

Knownly groups people into direction archetypes based on how they communicate, what they offer and what kind of online presence fits them best.

Archetype

Trusted Educator

Who this is

Someone who explains complex ideas clearly, often in a field like tech, finance, health, law or science.

What to create

Explainers, breakdowns, how-to content, myth-busting posts, beginner guides.

Avoid: oversimplifying for likes or creating clickbait educational content that lacks depth.
Archetype

Practical Authority Builder

Who this is

A professional or consultant who wants to be recognized for specific expertise within their industry.

What to create

Opinions, frameworks, case-based insights, lessons from real work, niche observations.

Avoid: generic industry content that sounds like everyone else in your field.
Archetype

Silent Expert

Who this is

Someone with deep expertise who prefers not to shout but wants selective, trust-based visibility.

What to create

Long-form writing, selective posts, curated insights, quiet thought leadership.

Avoid: forced virality strategies or high-volume posting that feels unnatural.
Archetype

Strategic Opportunity Spotter

Who this is

Someone who identifies patterns, trends and market shifts before others — and wants to be known for that foresight.

What to create

Trend analysis, predictions, market observations, opportunity-mapping content.

Avoid: sensationalism, hot takes without substance, or predicting everything.
Archetype

Founder-Led Voice

Who this is

A founder, entrepreneur or business owner who wants their point of view to build trust around their product or company.

What to create

Build-in-public content, founder lessons, product stories, customer-problem perspectives.

Avoid: over-selling, motivational noise, or faking a journey that does not reflect reality.
Archetype

Creative Story Educator

Who this is

Someone who teaches or shares through stories, examples, metaphors and creative framing rather than data or frameworks.

What to create

Stories, analogies, creative essays, experience-based lessons, narrative-driven content.

Avoid: telling stories without a clear point, or making everything about yourself.
